Index Laws

Index laws are the rules for simplifying expressions involving powers of the same base number.
They can be used to solve a variety of mathematical problems, and are essential for understanding many areas of mathematics, such as algebra, calculus, and trigonometry.

Index Law 1: Multiplication

This law states that when multiplying powers with the same base, add the indices.

am × an = am+n

For example:

23 × 25 = 23+5 = 28

Index Law 2: Division

This law states that when dividing powers with the same base, subtract the indices.

am ÷ an = am-n

For example:

23 ÷ 25 = 23-5 = 20

Index Law 3: Power of a Power

This law states that when raising a power to a power, multiply the indices.

(am)n = amn

For example:

(23)3 = 23*3 = 29

Index Law 4: Power of a Product

This law states that when raising a product to a power, distribute the power to each base.

(ab)m = am × bm

For example:

(2 × 3)2 = 22 × 32 = 4 × 9 = 36

Index Law 5: Power of a Quotient

This law states that when raising a quotient to a power, distribute the power to each base.

(a / b)m = am / bm

For example:

(2 / 3)2 = 22 / 32 = 4 / 9 ~ 0.44

Index Law 6: Zero Power

This law states that any number raised to the power of zero is equal to one.

a0 = 1

For example:

20 = 1

Note: The result of 00 is somewhat contested. See This wikipedia article

Index Law 7: Negative Powers

This law states that any number raised to a negative power is equal to the reciprocal of the number raised to the positive of that power./p>

a-n = 1⁄an

For example:

2-3 = 1⁄23 = 1⁄8

Index Law 8: Fractional Powers

Fractional powers can be thought of as roots. For example, the square root of 4 is 2, which can be written as 4(1/2).

a(m/n) = n√am

For example:

4(1/2) = √4 = 2

Conclusion

Index laws are a powerful tool for simplifying and solving mathematical expressions.
By understanding and using index laws, you can make your mathematical work much easier and more efficient.
